I know. I think of the fate of Bartek and Christine, Vatos and Alla, maybe the others now scattered over France. I even feel sorry for Tatiana, with the dark mark where the piano wire cut her throat. I just hope I will live long enough to see him again. When I find out what I am doing after tonight, I will search for him. I will find a way.

She reaches out for me, then everything fades.

Etienne, the owner wakes me. "Getaspo, they are coming. They got their informers here, but my informer is warning me to get out."

"But I thought the gendarmerie did . . ."

"They are cracking down hard. We must get you out, now."

I grab my bag and wipe my face. Perhaps some old rain water fell, because my cheeks are wet.

Etienne runs down the stairs, cranes his neck over the railing, and then runs back up into the attic. "They are coming out the main road, you can't get out this way!"

Louise glances at the large round window across from the door. All you can see is a sliver of moon and the dark sky. She points to the window and says, "We'll climb out here."

I look at Louise. "Do you really think we can do it?"

She tilts her head. "Have you got another idea, Daniel?"

I sigh. "Let's climb out the window then."

Etienne unlocks the blot and opens the window. It is now colder, with freezing wind blowing into the room. "I'll make up a story, just go!" With that, Etienne throws down whatever effects we brought with us. Louise grabs her basket with Grissette with a huff before he can throw it. Basket in one hand, Louise gets out first and I follow.

She makes steady time, grabbing the second story window ledge while she balances on the back door's frame. Grissette's basket dangles from her hand, but I am not sure how scared he is. After she lands on the ground, Grissette under her arm, I try to climb faster than she could. I jump down and grab my bag. We take off running just when we hear trucks and cars pull up.

Louise and I run across fields and into patches of forest. Truthfully, I do not know where we are going. I have no idea what safe houses are still safe or where to hide.

Just then, a truck barrels out of the road and into the forest. Just as I was about to run, I see a dark shape lean out of the truck window. I look closer; the truck is white, like the milk trucks. With petrol and milk rationing and exporting milk to Germany, there are fewer and fewer of these around. What is it doing here?

The dark shape then yells, "Hey, you! Hey Red and Girlie, you with Etienne?" Louise glares and folds her arms. I just notice that the words are in English. For lack of better options, I'll assume he is an ally.

I nod. The shape then opens the door and says "Get in, we got something for you to do." Taking one final look behind me, I sprint to the milk truck and jump in. Louise follows, albeit more cautiously. He directs to get behind boxes and starts driving.

Boxes are banging into us, but we stay still. Louise whispers prayers, but I have nothing to say. I don't know where I'm going. I don't know if I am coming back to Paris. I don't know where Tim is. I know only I miss him, and I'm cold.

"What happened to the others?"

"They're alright, they just broke up and went separate ways."

"Where's Danny? Is he alive?"

"He is alive, but he is driven to an assignment outside of Paris. That is all I can tell you, Mr. Burton."

"I told you not to call me that."

"I'm sorry--"

"Nom de Jesu, just call me Tim then."

"Well then, Tim, that is really all I can tell you."

"When will I see him again?"

"When his assignment is complete. When you tell us where we can find your father."

"You never told me why you need my father so badly. Did he do something wrong?"

"Yes. Yes, he did."
